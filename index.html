WHAT IS OPENSTREETMAP?

OpenStreetMap (OSM) is a free, community-built map of the world — like Wikipedia, but for maps. Our world generator pulls data directly from OSM to place benches, bike racks, trees, and street detail into Minecraft. The more OSM is filled in for Davis, the more alive our world will be. You don't need Minecraft — just a browser.

01
Create a free account
Go to openstreetmap.org and click Sign Up. Free — just needs an email.
Takes about 2 minutes
▶
02
Open Davis in editor
Click the button below — it opens OSM pre-loaded on Davis. Then click Edit → Edit with iD in the top bar.
Works in your browser — no download
▶
03
Add what you know
See a bench on a Davis street? Click Point, drop it on the map, choose "bench." Same for bike racks, trees, bins.
First edit takes under 5 minutes
▶
04
It appears in the world
At our next generation run, your edit is pulled in automatically. Your bench becomes a stone slab on a fence post — exactly where you placed it.
10+ edits = a special thanks in the credits
